# Mooseville Costume Creation Catalog

Purpose: This file defines costume sets for Mooseville characters and the main player.

Design intent:
- Keep every outfit moose-themed, practical, and tied to social identity.
- Provide male and female fit notes for each style.
- Keep silhouettes readable at third-person gameplay distance.
- Use layered parts so variants can be mixed for rewards and progression.

## Global Costume Direction

- Material palette: woven cloth, quilted canvas, leather straps, brushed metal accents, carved wood toggles.
- Color base: cedar brown, forest green, clay red, cream, charcoal, deep moss.
- Shape language: broad shoulders, antler-safe collars, hoof-friendly cuffs, utility belts and satchels.
- Cultural marker: every outfit includes one visible moose motif (antler stitch, hoof clasp, pinecone badge, or muck coin charm).

## Body Fit Standards (Male and Female)

Male fit notes:
- Slightly broader chest and shoulder pattern options.
- Heavier hem weighting for coats and over-vests.
- Wider belt rig options for tool loadouts.

Female fit notes:
- Tailored shoulder line with flexible chest seam options.
- Layered waist wraps and fitted utility sash options.
- Additional drape variants for ceremonial and social outfits.

Shared fit rules:
- Never restrict movement around antlers, neck, or forelimb range.
- All working outfits must support crouch, carry, and climb motion.
- Role insignia should remain visible from front and back camera angles.

## Reward and Unlock Guidance

- Relaxing styles: unlock through festivals, friendship tiers, or home upgrades.

- Rare recolors: unlock through special quests and town milestone achievements.

## 3D Asset Delivery Checklist

For each costume create:
- Base mesh with male and female fit adjustments.
- At least 3 color variants from the pack list.
- Normal map details for stitching, badges, and utility wear.
- Accessory set as separate mesh pieces (belts, satchels, mantles, badges).
- LOD versions for town crowd rendering.
- Thumbnail turntable renders: front, side, back, and detail close-up.
