## Campaign Arc Summary (Pre-Game to Current Day)

### Campaign I: The Fading Glow Crisis
- Timeline: Around year 4 to 5 after founding.
- Trigger: Regression in posture, speech, and cognition.
- Discovery: Green flow redirected by secondary rock slide.

### Campaign II: Green Water Recovery
- Goal: Retrieve enriched water and restore affected citizens.
- Outcome: Recovery works established in Mooseville with managed shard chambers.

### Campaign III: Far Side Arrivals
- Trigger: Awakened outsiders arrive in regressed state.
- Outcome: Stabilized with Moose Water; integration pathways established.

### Campaign IV: Age of Invitation
- Loop: Search teams find wild moose, offer aid, and respect join-or-return choice.
- Outcome: Population growth tied to town expansion and new specialization.

## Economy and Civic Rules (Gameplay)

- Currency: Mucks
- Earning: city work, quests, contracts, specialty sales
- Spending: non-essentials, upgrades, cosmetics, optional tools
- Free forever: Moose Water and basic food
- Shared duty: all citizens contribute to farming and gathering
- Trade layer: specialty foods and crafted goods can be sold

## Job Identity and Training

- Every citizen can request a role or receive assignment by town need.
- Role identity includes title, routine, social affinity, and outfit variants.
- Player progression unlocks teaching authority.
- NPC progression advances from novice to worker to journeymoose to specialist to mentor.

## Milestone-Driven Unlock Structure

- Quests unlock by town level, campaign progress, and staffing thresholds.
- Infrastructure scaling depends on resource throughput and trained workforce.
- Milestones M1 through M9 are tracked in Documents/Mooseville_Milestone_Roadmap.csv.

## Construction and Resource Throughput

- Buildings require delivered materials from citizens and businesses.
- Core chains include:
  - tree to logs to boards
  - rock to stone
  - clay to bricks
  - iron ore to bars to nails
- Manual production is slower but always available.
- Business production is faster but staffing dependent.

## Narrative Guardrails for Quest Design

- Care first: protect at-risk Moosians.
- Systems second: each major arc should permanently improve town capability.
- Consent always: recruitment is invitation-based, never forced.
- Identity always: solutions should remain culturally Moosian.
