# Section 1 — World Overview & Master Geography
## 1.1 The Valley — Master Description


Core geographic context for all level design, environment art, and world-building decisions. Read this section before placing any terrain, structure, or landmark asset.

The Valley is a mountain-ringed basin approximately 12km × 8km in total playable area. It is a closed geographic system — ringed by high peaks on all sides except the sealed eastern pass. The valley floor descends gently from north to south, with the Green Spring originating near the northern peak cluster and draining south through Mooseville into the Crystal Lake and then the southern marsh.
This geography means water — and with it, the influence of the sky-stone — flows through the entire living heart of the valley.

◆ Narrative Geography Note
The valley has never been seen in its entirety from above by any Moosian. Its full shape is known only from the highest ridge lookout, which shows only the inner basin. What lies beyond the peaks is unknown, except for what came through the eastern pass — and that pass has been sealed for 22 years.

# Section 2 — Mooseville Town — District Atlas
## 2.1 Town Overview & Philosophy

Mooseville is the player's home base and social hub. Its population is approximately 80–120 Moosians at game start. It grew organically from the First Gathering site near the Green Spring outflow, expanding district by district over 22 years. The architecture is a blend of hand-adapted human concepts (from the Sacred Documents) and Moosian invention: wide doorways, antler racks, hoof-friendly ramps, low wide tables, and curved wall niches designed for a moose's broad body.

◆ Town Layout Philosophy
Mooseville is NOT a grid. It grew along the natural path of the stream and the terrain. Streets curve around old cedar trees. Buildings cluster by function, but not rigidly. The central fire at the Heartfire District is always visible from any point in town — it is the visual and emotional anchor. This sight-line must be preserved in all environment art layouts.


## 2.2 District 1 — The Heartfire District


Function: Town Center, Civic Life, Community Gathering

◆ The Central Fire The original fire Melvin and Martha lit on the first cold night. Never extinguished in 22 years. Surrounded by carved log benches radiating outward. NPCs gather here every evening. This fire must always be rendering — no LOD culling at any viewable distance.

◆ The Gathering Hall Largest building in Mooseville. Carved timber frame, wide arched doorway (antler-clearance height), open interior with a central clay hearth, hanging woven banners — each representing a founding value: community, water, memory, shelter, growth. Used for festivals, votes, announcements, and emergency meetings.

◆ The Community Board Wooden announcement board outside the Gathering Hall. Displays active community quests, event announcements, new arrival notices, and seasonal calendar. Player checks this daily. Interactive object — text dynamically populated from quest state.

◆ Melvin & Martha's Home The founders' current residence, adjacent to the Heartfire. Larger than most homes, well-worn, full of artifacts. The player will visit this regularly for story beats. Mark as LD_StoryAnchor_MelvinMartha in engine.

◆ The Archive Hall Gloria Finch's lineage-managed hall of records. Houses Sacred Document copies, census records, hand-drawn maps, and the Wall of Names (founding members inscribed in order). Player's name added here at game's end.

Ambient Details: Smoke from the Central Fire rises visibly from anywhere in town. Children moose play around the benches in the afternoon. Elders gather for evening conversation. The sound of the nearby Green Spring outflow is audible as a gentle underscore.

## 2.3 District 2 — Muckmarket Row


Function: Commerce, Crafts, Specialty Food, Social Exchange

◆ The Muck Exchange A carved wooden market building where players can sell surplus goods (crops, fish, crafted items) for Mucks and browse specialty goods unavailable elsewhere.

◆ Market Stalls (6–8 Rotating) Individual vendors set up daily with different inventory. Some are permanent residents; others are new arrivals running their first stall. Stall placement and vendor schedules driven by dynamic NPC system.

◆ The Tasting Corner Marjorie Bell's secondary location — a small outdoor counter where she offers free daily tastings of a featured dish. Interacting here gives players a seasonal recipe hint.

◆ The Clothier Stall Sells cosmetic clothing items, dyeable fabrics, accessory pieces. Cosmetic system integration required.

◆ The Rarity Rack One stall run by Chester Lang's apprentice that sells rare crafting materials not found in the wilderness. Inventory reset schedule: weekly (in-game time).

Ambient Details: Busy morning and midday. Quieter evenings. The sound of bartering, wood carving, and friendly argument. Gold-painted wooden Muck coins hang decoratively from stall awnings.

## 2.4 District 3 — The Growing Circle


Function: Agriculture, Greenhouses, Seed Keeping, Community Food Supply

◆ Doris Vale's Farm The largest agricultural plot. A working farm managed by Doris Vale's descendants. Player can volunteer here for relationship points and crop-type knowledge.

◆ The Seed Archive A small stone-floored building where all known crop seeds are catalogued and stored. Players can purchase rare seeds; donating new seed varieties discovered in the wilderness adds to the archive and updates the catalogue UI.

◆ Community Greenhouse A glass-and-timber greenhouse (constructed Year 8 post-Starfall) for winter crop growing. Player can access for crafting cold-weather variants. Glass material must maintain interior warmth visual even during winter exterior conditions.

◆ Player's Personal Plot Adjacent to the Growing Circle (attached to the inherited homestead). Starts at 6×6 tile growing area; expands to 12×12 at full upgrade. Grid system with seasonal soil variation.

◆ The Irrigation Channel Stone-lined water channel running Moose Water from the Enhancement Works through the Growing Circle. Visible flowing green-tinted water in channels. Beautiful at night — add glow emitter pass to channel material.

Ambient Details: Soil smell, tool sounds, bees, birdsong. Sunrise light hits this district perfectly — golden rays through greenhouse glass. Doris Vale's lineage NPC can always be found here in the mornings.

## 2.5 District 4 — The Archive Quarter


Function: Records, Education, History, Sacred Documents

◆ Gloria Finch's Archive Hall The official Archive. Has two rooms: the Public Reading Room (anyone may enter) and the Secure Document Room (relationship Level 3+ with Gloria's lineage NPC required). Gate state managed via relationship system flag.

◆ The Sacred Documents Display Case The original trunk found at the human campsite is preserved here, behind glass. The recovered books are laid open to key pages. New players can read these for lore. Interaction triggers journal entry auto-write.

◆ The Map Room A separate alcove within the Archive filled with hand-drawn maps of the valley made by Vincent Reed and successors. The player's exploration adds to the master map displayed here. Live map update system — reflect player discovery state in rendered texture.

◆ The Inscription Wall Stone wall with names of all known Moosians, by arrival order. Founding eight names are carved deepest; newer names are lighter. Player's name ends the wall — added in end-game sequence.

Ambient Details: Quiet. Dusty. Warm lamp light (oil lamps modeled after a device from the Sacred Documents). The scent of old paper and cedar. NPCs here whisper and read.

## 2.6 District 5 — The Docks & Drying Racks


Function: Fishing, Water Management, Secondary Moose Water Distribution

◆ Town Creek Dock Small wooden dock on the outflow creek where town-accessible fishing occurs. Starting fishing location for the player. Entry-level fish spawn table active here from Day 1.

◆ Fish Drying Racks Long wooden racks where caught fish are hung to cure. Visual landmark communicating community food production. Fish assets populate dynamically based on recent catch events.

◆ Harold Winslow's Water Station The meteorological and hydrological observation post. Weather predictions, flood warnings, and seasonal river condition reports are posted here. Harold's lineage NPC present most mornings. Interface for weather forecast system.

◆ The Net Shed Crafting location for fishing gear (rods, nets, bait containers). Requires Craft Knack Tier 1 to use advanced workbench inside.

Ambient Details: Running water sound constant. Morning mist. Frogs. Birds diving for fish. The smell of fresh water and cedar planks.

## 2.7 District 6 — The Repair Yard


Function: Crafting, Building, Tool Repair, Salvage Workshop

◆ Chester Lang's Workshop The primary advanced crafting location. Full range of tools and workbenches. Chester's lineage NPC can teach the player recipes not learnable elsewhere. Upgrade state modifies available workbench tiers visually.

◆ The Salvage Pile A constantly shifting pile of recovered materials from wilderness exploration and demolition. Players can sort through it once per day for free material finds. Random loot table, refresh daily.

◆ The Build Yard Open outdoor workspace where Raymond Harlow's lineage NPC coordinates town construction projects. Player can volunteer to assist for relationship points and materials.

◆ The Tool Wall Display of every tool type used in Mooseville, built as a teaching reference from Sacred Document illustrations. Clicking any tool gives lore description. Interactive UI element with 18 distinct tool entries.

Ambient Details: Hammering, sawing, fire from the forge. Busy all day. Chester's apprentices move materials. The smell of cut wood and metal.

## 2.8 District 7 — The Player's Homestead


Function: Player Base, Personal Crafting, Personal Farm, Rest Point

◆ The Original Home Built by Melvin and Martha as their first permanent structure. Now gifted to the player. Three rooms at start (main room, sleeping alcove, small storage). Expandable to 6 rooms. Room expansion modifies mesh and interior layout dynamically.

◆ Personal Crafting Station Tier 1 available from day one. Upgradeable to Tier 3 through materials + Raymond's lineage NPC relationship. Each tier adds new mesh attachments and available recipe categories.

◆ The Green Spring View The homestead has a clear sightline to the original Green Spring — intentional by Melvin and Martha. On clear nights, the spring's glow illuminates the homestead windows. Confirm sight-line is unobstructed at all vegetation LOD levels.

◆ The Memory Wall Inside the homestead, a wall where old photographs (hand-drawn portraits), Melvin's tools, and Martha's first written records are displayed. Story items found by the player can be added here. Dynamic display — each placed item updates the wall composition.

Ambient Details: Home sounds — wind on wooden walls, fire crackling, birdsong through open windows. This is the player's safest and calmest space. Reverb preset: Indoor Warm Domestic.

## 2.9 District 8 — The Enhancement Works


Function: Moose Water Production, Civic Distribution

◆ The Shard Station A stone basin system where water passes through chambers containing secured sky-stone shards, taking on the beneficial trace. The original engineering design by Harold Winslow, Year 5 post-Starfall. Sky-stone visual effect active in water material shader.

◆ The Distribution Tap Public tap where any Moosian (including the player) may fill containers with Moose Water, free of charge, up to the daily civic limit. Interaction: fill container. State resets at dawn.

◆ The Shard Observation Window A glass viewing panel where sky-stone shards can be seen glowing green through the water flow. One of the most visually iconic spots in town. Highest priority for visual polish. Glow emitter: #1B5E20 with bloom, intensity increases at night.

◆ The Civic Notice Post Emergency notices specific to Enhancement Works health. Degradation warnings during Act 1 are posted here first. Text dynamically populated from narrative event triggers.

Ambient Details: The sound of flowing water over stone. A faint, warm green glow at all hours, brightest at dusk and night. The most visited location in Mooseville after the Central Fire.

# Section 3 — Wilderness Biomes — Detailed Atlas
## 3.1 Biome Design Philosophy


Each biome is a distinct emotional register. Moving between them should feel like turning a page. No biome is hostile — each is inviting in a different way. The wilderness is not a danger zone; it is a wonder zone. Obstacles are environmental, not aggressive.

The biome system is the primary vehicle for environmental storytelling. Each biome must deliver a unique sensory signature — in lighting, sound, material palette, vegetation density, and sky framing. Transitions between biomes should be gradual (a 30–50m blend zone) but unmistakable in character.


◆
Biome 1 — Emerald Forest


Emotional Register
Ancient, sheltered, close, watchful
Size
~1.8km × 1.2km (northwest quadrant)
Elevation
Gently rising; 20–60m above town level
Access
Open from game start
Season Variation
Bright spring green → dense summer canopy → amber/rust autumn → deep snow-laden winter (most dramatic winter transformation)

### Terrain Description
Dense old-growth pine, cedar, and fir. Canopy so thick that sunlight enters only in shafts and patches. Fern hollows fill every open floor space. Fallen nurse logs create habitat for fungi, insects, and small animals. The forest floor is soft — almost soundless walking. Underground root networks cause occasional ground-surface texture variation.
### Key Landmarks
◆ The First Path The original trail worn by moose hooves before awakening. Still visible as a subtle depression in the forest floor. Melvin and Martha walked this path every morning for years. Now a meditation walk for elder Moosians. Tag: Landmark_The_FirstPath

◆ The Hollow Cedar A massive cedar tree with a lightning-carved hollow large enough for a moose to stand inside. Used historically as emergency shelter. Now a known meeting spot — NPCs leave notes here. Notes are readable interactive objects.

◆ The Nurse Log Gallery A sequence of seven enormous fallen logs in a rough line, each supporting its own micro-ecosystem of moss, ferns, fungi, and small creatures. Primary foraging hot spot. Each log has a unique material and vegetation cluster.

◆ Bear Family Territory Northwest quadrant. Home to a bear family (two adults, one or two cubs depending on season). Bears are non-threatening. At Closeness Level 1 (via non-aggressive interaction), they become landmark companions — present at predictable times. Schedule driven by wildlife AI system.

◆ The Amber Glade A natural clearing where the canopy opens and golden-hour light floods in. The only place in the forest with full sunlight. Deer visit at dawn. One of the most visually spectacular locations for any time of day. Wayrest Point: The Forest Hollow.

◆ Sky-Stone Trace #1 (Dormant) A faint green vein visible in a rocky outcrop near the northern forest edge. Activatable with Spring Knack Tier 2 and Moose Water. Tag: SS_Deposit_1_Dormant. Lore fragment: impression of impact, heat, and speed — the Starfall's moment of arrival.

◆
Biome 2 — Golden Meadow


Emotional Register
Open, warm, joyful, alive, spacious

~2.5km × 1.5km (east, curving southeast)

Near valley floor; 5–15m above town level

### Terrain Description
The most open biome in the valley. Rolling grassland interrupted by occasional rocky outcrops, lone trees, and low shrubs. Grass height varies — knee-height on a moose in spring, shoulder-height in late summer. Wind is constant and visible in the grass. The meadow provides the only unobstructed view of the sky in the valley, making it the best stargazing location.
### Key Landmarks
◆ The Lone Oak A massive solitary oak tree at the meadow's center. Completely anomalous — oaks don't grow here naturally. A long-standing mystery with a lore payoff in Act 2 (a Sacred Document page reveals a human planted it as a survey marker). Player can picnic under it. NPCs have carved initials in the bark. Wayrest Point: The Meadow Marker.

◆ The Survey Stake Cluster Three rusted metal stakes with faded orange survey ribbon still attached. The oldest human remnants encountered early in the game. Interact to begin the human mystery thread. Journal entry auto-written on interaction. Tag: QT_HumanMystery_01_Discover

◆ The Wildflower Meadow In spring, a half-kilometer swath of wildflowers blooms in rings of color. Used for flower-gathering quests, gifting materials, and festival decoration sourcing. Vegetation density driven by season override volume.

◆ The Deer Crossing At dawn and dusk, a herd of deer crosses the eastern meadow between forest edge and rocky ridge. Non-interactive but beautiful — a camera-worthy moment the game draws attention to via a soft UI hint on first occurrence.

◆ The Star Watching Flat A wide flat area near meadow center, slightly elevated, with no tree obstruction above. NPCs hold informal stargazing nights here. Key location for night lore events tied to the constellation mystery. Night sky must be fully rendered from this position — no billboard vegetation occlusion overhead.

◆ The Old Fence Line A barely visible row of rusted wire and rotten posts stretching 200m. More human remnants. Suggests the meadow was once bordered or parceled. Lore fragment available in Act 2.

◆ Sky-Stone Trace #2 (Surface) A scatter of small glowing fragments embedded in the soil, exposed by spring rainstorms. Collectible sky-stone shards for Enhancement Works upgrade in Act 2. Tag: SS_Deposit_2_Active

◆
Biome 3 — Crystal Lakeshore


Emotional Register
Serene, reflective, sacred, magical, still
Size
Lake ~800m diameter; playable lakeshore ~2km circumference
Elevation
10m below town level (water table sink)
Access
Open from start; inner shore/deep fishing unlocked with Trail Knack Tier 2
Season Variation
Mist-covered mornings in spring → crystal-clear summer → deep amber autumn reflections → partially frozen edges in winter

### Terrain Description
The valley's primary water body. Water clarity is exceptional — from shore, the bottom is visible to 4m depth. In the shallows (0–1m), green-tinged water reveals sky-stone mineral trace from spring inflow. Shoreline alternates between sandy patches, smooth stone flats, and reed beds. The eastern shore has a rock shelf above the waterline used as a natural observation platform. A small island (30m diameter) sits in the lake center — accessible only by crafted raft (Trail Knack Tier 3 or Craft Knack Tier 2).
### Key Landmarks
◆ The Green Shallows The northwestern shore where spring inflow enters the lake. Water glows faintly green at the edge, day and night. Sky-stone trace clearly visible in substrate. Non-drinkable from the lake (diluted). One of the most photographed in-game locations. High-priority water shader polish.

◆ The Stone Dock A natural flat rock ledge used as the primary fishing platform. Harold's lineage brings a weather instrument kit here each morning. Best location for 8 of the 24 fish species. Wayrest Point: The Shore Stone.

◆ The Reed Beds (North Shore) Dense cattail and reed growth. Harvesting materials for weaving and crafting. Waterfowl nesting — disturbing nests has a relationship consequence with wildlife-caring NPCs. Nest disturbance tracked by interaction flag.

◆ The Observation Rock (East Shore) A flat-topped boulder 3m above water level. Best view of the full lake and mountain reflections. Player journal entries written here have special illustrated formatting — trigger journal style override on interaction.

◆ Center Island Small wooded island. Contains a rusted metal lockbox (human remnant — Act 2 quest), one unique fish species habitat, and a strange circular stone arrangement of unknown origin. Raft crafting requirement gate enforced by trigger volume.

◆ The Melt Pool (High Water, Spring Only) A temporary pool that forms on the northwest shore during spring snowmelt. Hosts 2 rare fish species for a 2-week in-game window only. Seasonal spawn condition: Season == Spring && Week <= 2.

◆ Sky-Stone Trace #3 (Submerged) Visible from above in summer (clearest water). Requires crafted diving weight to reach. Spring Knack Tier 3 activates it. Tag: SS_Deposit_3_Dormant. Lore: impression of cold, silence, depth — the sky-stone sensing the water.

◆
Biome 4 — The Marsh Shallows


Emotional Register
Slow, ancient, quiet, slightly mysterious, alive-underfoot
Size
~1.5km × 1km (southwest quadrant)
Elevation
Lowest point in the valley; 5m below town level
Access
Open from start; deeper areas require Craft Knack Tier 2 (wading boots)

### Terrain Description
The southern wetland carved by beavers before awakening. Still-active beaver dams on the western edge maintain water levels. The marsh is navigable via raised root paths and old moose-worn raised ground. Water is 0.1–0.5m deep in most traversable areas; deeper sections blocked by thick reed walls. Rich in wildlife and plant life. A sense of deep time — this part of the valley has changed least since before the Starfall.
### Key Landmarks
◆ The Beaver Dams Two active beaver dams on the west marsh. Beavers are present (non-awakened; pure wildlife). Their maintenance of the dams maintains marsh water levels — a point of ecology mentioned by Harold's lineage NPC. Water level system responds to dam state.

◆ The Raised Root Paths Natural elevated root networks from old-growth marsh trees forming navigable walkways. Following them leads to inner marsh areas. Navigation aid — paths subtly highlighted when Closeness Level 1 with marsh-associated NPC is achieved.

◆ The Sunken Log Platform An enormous old log, half-submerged, forming a natural raft-like platform. Used as a resting spot and secondary fishing location. Custom physics: slight float response to player weight.

◆ The Oldest Tree A massive bald cypress at the marsh's center — estimated pre-Starfall age of 200+ years. The First Circle held their first formal meeting under this tree (Year 2). A carved marker at its base commemorates this. Wayrest Point: The Marsh Root.

◆ Sky-Stone Trace #4 (Buried) Beneath the marsh substrate. Not accessible until Act 3 — marsh draining is a quest event that reveals this deposit. Tag: SS_Deposit_4_Buried. Locked by: QT_Act3_MarshDrain_Complete.



◆
Biome 5 — Ancient Ruins


Emotional Register
Awe, mystery, old silence, slightly unsettling (not threatening), discovery
Size
Ruin complex ~600m × 400m; surrounding wilderness 1.5km buffer
Elevation
120m above town (natural rock plateau, northwest)
Access
Trail Knack Tier 1 required (stamina for climb); partial access from start

### Terrain Description
A natural stone plateau overgrown with moss, lichen, and low scrub. The "ruins" are not traditional buildings — they are human-built structures collapsed into weathered stone frames, rusted metal frames, and concrete pads reclaimed by vegetation. At first glance they appear as odd natural formations. On closer inspection, the regularity becomes unmistakable: this was constructed. By whom? For what?
### Key Landmarks
◆ The Survey Station (Ruins Site A) The most intact structure — a collapsed cabin frame with a partial roof of rusted corrugated metal. Inside: a weathered folding table, three metal stakes still upright, and a rusted canister containing the most intact human-written pages found outside the original trunk. Act 1 quest destination. Wayrest Point: The Ruin Shelf.

◆ The Equipment Cache (Ruins Site B) A buried cache revealed by the Act 1 tremor. Contains human tools (measuring instruments, a compass, glass vials). Each item brought to the Archive yields a lore entry. Inventory items: discoverable post-tremor event only.

◆ The Foundation Grid A series of concrete pad corners in a 4×4 grid pattern (16 pads total). Suggests a planned structure never completed — or demolished. Grid alignment points directly toward the eastern pass. This alignment must be preserved exactly in level geometry.

◆ The Moss Inscription On a flat stone slab, barely visible under moss. A carved grid of letters and numbers. Translation quest (Archive-based) reveals it as survey coordinates pointing to the High Shelf impact site. Text revealed progressively as translation quest advances.

◆ Eagle Lookout Eagles nest here. At Closeness Level 2 with the eagle pair (via sustained respectful presence), one eagle will guide the player toward hidden items in the Ruins and High Shelf areas. Eagle guidance system: directional flight animation toward target.

Ambient Soundscape: Wind across stone, hawk calls, distant creek sound, occasional dislodged pebble. Quietest biome — almost meditative.


◆
Biome 6 — The High Shelf


Emotional Register
Vast, exposed, humbling, sacred, the top of the world
Size
Plateau ~800m × 600m; impact crater 150m diameter
Elevation
280m above town (highest accessible point in the game)
Access
Trail Knack Tier 2 + Craft Knack Tier 2 (climbing equipment); Act 2 quest unlocks path fully

### Terrain Description
A high mountain plateau reached via a steep switchback trail accessible after clearing a small rockslide (Act 2 quest). The plateau is above the treeline — exposed, windswept, with scattered alpine plants, lichen-covered boulders, and snow patches year-round. The impact crater from the Starfall is clearly visible: a bowl-shaped depression approximately 150m across, 20m deep at center, with shattered and fused rock at the rim.
### Key Landmarks
◆ The Impact Crater The origin point of the Starfall. The crater floor contains the highest concentration of sky-stone in the valley. Shards visible on surface. The crater glows green at night — visible from Mooseville as a distant light on clear nights, though no Moosian has known what it was until the player discovers it. Night glow emitter: distant light actor at crater center, radius 500m.

◆ The Primary Sky-Stone Deposit Main deposit at crater center. Interacting (Spring Knack Tier 3) triggers the deepest and longest lore vision in the game — the full Starfall moment, the sky-stone's origin, and the first hint of intentionality. Act 2 climax. Cinematic trigger on interaction. Tag: SS_Deposit_Primary_Dormant

◆ The Human Flag Post A metal pole, corroded but still standing, at the plateau edge. A fragment of faded cloth still attached. The significance becomes clear in Act 3 — this was a survey team's claim marker. Interactive lore object; full context unlocked in Act 3.

◆ The Valley Overlook The only point in the game from which the full valley interior is visible. A mandatory story moment — the player sees Mooseville from above for the first time. Deeply emotional beat. Cinematic camera: pan from impact crater to valley overlook on first visit. Cutscene flag: HS_ValleyOverlook_Seen.

◆ The High Shelf Melt Pool A permanent snowmelt pool near the impact crater, fed by year-round snowfields. Contains 4 unique fish species found nowhere else (night fishing only). Water has the highest sky-stone trace of any accessible water body. Pool water shader: highest intensity sky-stone glow variant.

Biome 7 — The Sealed Eastern Pass


Emotional Register
Tension, anticipation, blockage, the threshold

Access
Act 2 crack event opens narrow entry; full traversal in Act 3

### Terrain Description
The original migration route through the eastern mountain range, sealed by the Starfall landslide. The pass is a narrow canyon between two peak faces, filled with consolidated debris: granite boulders, ice chunks (still frozen 22 years later in the canyon shadow), uprooted trees compacted into fill, and fine debris cemented by freeze-thaw cycles. The landslide fill is not loose — it has become its own geological formation. Crossing is not dangerous in-game, but atmospherically heavy. This is where the old world ended.
### Key Landmarks
◆ The Landslide Face The wall of consolidated debris visible from the eastern meadow for 22 years. On close inspection, fragments of the sky-stone impact shockwave are still visible as fused sections of rock. Visible from Golden Meadow — must read as a distant visual landmark at biome boundary range.

◆ The Act 2 Crack A fissure opened by the Act 1 tremor, wide enough for a single moose to squeeze through sideways. The player is the first Moosian to enter since the Starfall. Geometry gated by Act 2 narrative state flag. Squeeze animation trigger on entry.

◆ The Echo Chamber A natural void within the debris where sound behaves strangely — voices echo in an unsettling delay. NPC dialogue here has unique reverb treatment. Audio: Reverb Preset = Echo Chamber (custom). Delay: 0.4s, Decay: 3.0s.

◆ The First Crossing View Emerging from the pass into the far side — the moment the player sees the Far Valley for the first time. One of the most significant visual moments in the entire game. Mandatory cinematic camera on exit trigger.



◆
Biome 8 — The Far Valley (Act 3 Only)


Emotional Register
Unknown, expansive, different, mirror-image, hope
Size
Limited Act 3 exploration zone ~3km × 2km (not fully mapped)
Access
Act 3 exclusive — full pass traversal required

### Description
The far side of the eastern pass reveals a second valley — smaller than the main valley, with different vegetation (more open, fewer conifers, more birch and aspen), a second stream, and clear signs of a separate awakened moose community — primitive early settlement, reminiscent of Mooseville's Year 1–2 state. This is where the "Far Side Arrivals" from the lore originated.
A second sky-stone deposit is visible on the far valley's western cliff face. And at the far valley's northern end, a second sealed pass with something glowing on its other side — the final unresolved mystery, left for a future sequel.

◆ Design Note — Act 3 Scope
The Far Valley exploration zone is intentionally limited in Act 3 scope — it is a discovery, not a second full game world. Do not over-build. The emotional impact comes from possibility and implication, not complete revelation. The second sealed pass at the far valley's north is a visual landmark only — not traversable in v1.0.

# Section 4 — The Old Campsite — Human Remnant Site

Classification
Points of Interest, non-biome (located within Emerald Forest western edge)
Emotional Register
Archaeological wonder, unsettling recognition, narrative weight
Access
Optional discovery — no quest marker. Player-initiated exploration only.

## Description

The original abandoned campsite discovered by the First Gathering. A rise above a creek bend, now heavily overgrown. The original Sacred Documents trunk has been removed to the Archive — but the campsite itself remains.
Players who find it — entirely optionally, with no quest marker directing them — discover the following scene:
- The outline of a tent footprint in the soil (visible as a subtle rectangular depression)
- A rusted fuel canister (interactable — yields lore entry on human fuel technology)
- A collapsed folding chair (barely recognizable; yields lore entry on human furniture)
- A glass lens (optic fragment) half-buried (yields lore entry; hints at human optical instruments)
- A plaque left by the First Circle: "Here we found memory. Here we became more." (carved in Moosian script)

◆ Organic Discovery — Design Priority
The campsite is a deeply lore-rich location. All of Mooseville's culture traces back to this single point. Finding it without a quest prompt is one of the game's most rewarding organic discovery moments. Do not add a map marker, waypoint, or quest indicator under any circumstances. The player must find this on their own. The only signal is the natural visual curiosity of an overgrown clearing with a distinctive rock formation at its edge. Level design must preserve this.

## 6.3 In-Game Map Aesthetic

The player's map is rendered as a parchment-toned hand-drawn illustration matching Mooseville's lore world of physical cartography. This is not a satellite view or HUD minimap. It is a living artifact that reflects the world as the Moosians understand it.
- Fill behavior: The map fills in progressively as the player physically explores. Unexplored areas are blank parchment — no silhouettes, no fog-of-war gradients.
- Landmark icons: Named landmarks appear with small illustrated icons in a carved-wood style. Icons are designed by the UI team in the landmark icon style guide (separate document).
- Wayrest markers: Each discovered Wayrest Point is marked with a compass rose symbol rendered in gold (#FFD700).
- Player position: Indicated by a small moose-antler silhouette stamp in deep green (#1B5E20).
- Map update trigger: Discovery events write to the exploration state array. Map texture updates are batched on scene transition to avoid mid-scene redraws.
- The Map Room effect: When the player's exploration data matches what is displayed in the Archive Map Room, a special lore moment triggers — the hand-drawn map in the Archive is updated to show the player's name as the cartographer.

◆ Art Direction — Map Texture Style
The map texture is hand-illustrated by the UI art team and updated via a masking system driven by exploration state data. Do not use procedural map generation. The parchment quality, ink-line style, and icon illustration must remain consistent with the Mooseville aesthetic — warm, handmade, imperfect, and full of love. Reference: Map_StyleGuide_v1.0.pdf (UI team documentation).
