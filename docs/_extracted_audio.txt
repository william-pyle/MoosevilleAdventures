# Section 1: Audio Vision & Philosophy
## 1.1 The Mooseville Sound Promise

Every sound in Mooseville Adventures — from the creak of the Gathering Hall door to the full orchestral swell of a Legacy Moment — should feel like it belongs to the same hand-crafted, living world. Audio is not decoration in Mooseville. It is memory. It is the way the player feels the difference between standing in the Emerald Forest at dawn and standing on the High Shelf in a winter wind.
Audio is how NPCs feel present even when off-screen. Audio is how the Green Spring communicates that something here is older and stranger than ordinary water.

Core Principle:
In Mooseville Adventures, audio is a primary narrative layer — not a secondary feedback system. Every asset that enters this game must earn its place in the world's acoustic identity.

## 1.2 Three Audio Pillars


Pillar 1 — "Acoustic First"
Mooseville's world is built by hand with natural materials. Its soundscape must reflect this. The foundation of every biome's ambient layer is acoustic, organic, and real-world recorded: genuine forest ambience, actual water movement, wind across stone. Synthesized and processed elements are layered on top only where the world requires something beyond nature — the sky-stone, the Green Spring's glow, magical Moose Water interactions.


Pillar 2 — "Music That Breathes"
The musical score of Mooseville is adaptive and layered. It responds to time of day, player activity, emotional narrative weight, season, and biome. Music never starts or stops abruptly — it breathes in and out. The player should rarely be aware of the music system working; they should only feel the emotional result.


Pillar 3 — "Sound Tells the Story"
Audio cues in Mooseville carry narrative information that the visual layer sometimes does not. The Enhancement Works makes a specific harmonic hum — and when it begins to degrade, that hum subtly changes in a way the attentive player will notice before any quest reveals it. The eastern pass, when the Act 2 crack first opens, makes a sound that does not exist anywhere else in the valley. Sound is a narrative tool, not a feedback layer.

# Section 2: Music System
## 2.1 Adaptive Music Architecture

Mooseville uses a fully adaptive music system implemented in UE5 MetaSounds and Quartz. Music is organized into three layers that can be independently faded, crossfaded, swapped, and blended in real time.

Key Design Rule:
All three layers share the same root key and tempo at all times. Transitions between states within each layer use pre-written stem transitions — not crossfades — to ensure musical integrity. No hard cuts. No jarring handoffs.

## 2.2 Musical Identity — Instrumentation Palette

### Core Instruments (always present in some form)
- Acoustic guitar (fingerpicked, warm mid-register) — the melodic heartbeat of Mooseville; associated with home, community, and the player character
- Cello (solo or small section) — emotional depth; associated with legacy, memory, and Melvin/Martha
- Recorder / wooden flute — lightness, nature, discovery; leads melodic lines in exploration
- Hand percussion (frame drum, hand pan) — rhythm in active moments; natural and organic
- Hammered dulcimer — bright, folk-instrument warmth; associated with festival, market, community events
- String quartet — emotional weight for Legacy Moments and Act climaxes
### Textural Elements (added contextually)
- Crystal singing bowls — Green Spring presence; sky-stone activation; night ambience
- Prepared piano (subtle) — human remnant discovery; Ancient Ruins; unsettling-beautiful moments
- Deep bowed metal (e.g., waterphone or similar) — the sealed eastern pass; threshold moments
- Wordless vocals (single voice, female) — Legacy Moments; Starfall memorial sequences

Instruments Never Used in Mooseville:
•  Electric guitar, bass, or synthesizers with obvious digital character
•  Orchestral brass (too martial — Mooseville has no combat)
•  Heavy percussion (drums/snare — too aggressive)
•  Pure electronic/EDM textures

Impact Crater Special Sound:
Standing at the crater rim, the sky-stone deposit produces a resonant chord (stacked perfect 5ths: E–B–F#) that pulses slowly (once per 4 seconds). This is the most sustained and complex sky-stone audio in the game. During the Spring Knack Tier 3 activation sequence, the chord expands through 7 additional harmonics in sequence — a musical revelation.

The Echo Chamber — Audio Design Priority Location:
Voices spoken here produce a 0.8-second delay echo. The music system drops all active layers — the player hears only their footsteps and the canyon. When the pass opens, a single long drone plays for 12 seconds — the only time in the game audio makes a "door opening" metaphor this literally.

# Section 8: Audio Pipeline & Delivery Workflow
## 8.1 Audio Production Pipeline

- Design Brief — Audio Designer writes a brief per asset category specifying emotional target, material reference, delivery format, and variation count.
- Composition / Recording — Composer or Sound Designer produces source assets in DAW; delivered as 48kHz/24-bit WAV stems.
- Review — Audio Lead reviews against Tone Reference Guide (Section 1.3) and rejects anything that conflicts with the acoustic-first philosophy.
- Integration Prep — Assets renamed to convention; MetaSound patches built or updated; parameter hooks confirmed in Blueprint.
- UE5 Integration — Assets imported; MetaSound Sources configured; attenuation profiles assigned; reverb zones verified.
- In-Engine QA — Audio QA checklist run (see Section 8.2).
- Approval — Audio Lead sign-off before content freeze.
## 8.2 Audio QA Checklist (per asset batch)


☐  All files match naming convention
☐  No audio clips at 0dBFS (master peaks at -1dBFS or lower)
☐  No audible loop points on any ambient loop
☐  Footsteps trigger correctly on all 8 surface types
☐  Music layer transitions occur on Quartz 4-bar boundary
☐  Enhancement Works hum pitch shifts correctly with DegradationLevel parameter
☐  Sky-stone resonance activates and deactivates cleanly with Spring Knack tier
☐  NPC audio signatures identifiable from 10m without visual
☐  No audio voice budget exceeded during stress test (all NPCs in Heartfire District simultaneously)
☐  Reverb zone transitions produce no audio pop or abrupt change
☐  Echo Chamber delay parameter verified at 800ms
☐  UI sounds pass through without spatialization (confirmed 2D)
☐  Seasonal music filter applies and removes without audible artifact

## 8.3 Prohibited Audio Practices


✗  No audio asset delivered as MP3 at any stage of production
✗  No single-variation footstep systems (minimum 6 per surface)
✗  No music that hard-cuts (all transitions must be quantized or faded)
✗  No synthetic percussion in any biome ambience
✗  No electric or heavily synthesized instrument in the score
✗  No UI sound that uses a pure sine wave or harsh digital beep
✗  No NPC ambient audio that loops at under 5 seconds
✗  No audio volume settings that allow voices to clip without limiting



# Section 9: Future Audio Considerations
## 9.1 Voice Acting (Post-Launch Consideration)

Mooseville Adventures v1.0 ships without full voice acting. NPC audio is handled through ambient presence sounds, musical emotional cues, and written dialogue. A voice acting pass is planned for a post-launch update, with the following design constraints defined now to guide future implementation:
- All Moosian voices should feel warm, natural, and slightly unhurried
- Melvin's voice direction: low-mid range; deliberate; occasional dry warmth
- Martha's voice direction: clear, precise, emotional depth beneath professional tone
- No cartoony or exaggerated vocal styles — this is not a children's cartoon; it is a warm family story
- Voice lines will be implemented via MetaSound Voice Integration patch (patch reserved: MS_Voice_NPC)

Implementation Note:
The
MS_Voice_NPC
patch slot is reserved in the current MetaSounds architecture. All NPC Blueprint components already include the voice parameter hook. No architectural rebuild will be required at post-launch voice integration.

## 9.2 Dynamic Music Expansion

Future biome additions — if the Far Valley is expanded in a sequel or DLC — should extend the Biome State enum in BP_MV_MusicManager. All Far Valley music should use the "Lore of the Valley" theme as its foundation, harmonized into an unfamiliar key.

Recommended Key for Far Valley:
F-sharp major — same harmonic DNA as the Valley theme but a half-step brighter, signaling a world that is related but not identical. The player's ear will feel the familiarity and the difference simultaneously.

## 9.3 Accessibility Audio Pass

A full accessibility audio pass is planned for post-launch to add:
- Visual waveform representation of significant audio cues — for deaf and hard-of-hearing players to perceive narrative audio events through visual feedback
- Colorblind-compatible audio cue indicator icons in HUD — distinct shapes alongside any color-dependent audio cue indicators
- Full mono compatibility test pass — verifying that no critical stereo-only content is lost when mono audio is enabled

Accessibility Design Principle:
No narrative-critical information should ever exist exclusively in audio without a visual fallback option. The Enhancement Works hum narrative signal is the primary example — the "Audio narrative cues" toggle in Section 5.3 provides the visual fallback for this specific case. All future audio-narrative signals should be designed with the same toggle pattern.
