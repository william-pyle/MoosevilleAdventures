Art Direction

Visual Style Guide

Complete Mooseville Rendering Bible — color system, shaders, cel shading, outlines, LOD specs, material language, lighting, VFX, and all visual standards.

1. Visual Pillars

Emerald Alive — The green world of Mooseville is never static, never flat, never cold. Foliage shifts in the breeze, moss catches dappled light, and the deep emerald palette breathes with warm undertones of gold and amber. Every environment asset must contribute to the sense of a living, photosynthesizing world.
Golden Hour Always — Mooseville's lighting defaults to the warmth and directionality of the golden hour regardless of in-world time of day. Even noon scenes carry a slight warm cast; even night scenes use amber torch-glow as the primary light source. The sun of Mooseville is always generous and inviting.
Storybook Legibility — Every character, prop, and landmark must be immediately readable in silhouette and color at all expected gameplay distances. Cel-shading bands, outlines, and saturated palette choices are all in service of clarity. Mooseville never sacrifices readability for photorealism.
Tactile Warmth — Surfaces in Mooseville feel hand-made. Wood looks carved, stone looks stacked by careful hands, fabric looks woven. The material language must communicate craft, care, and age — the sense that someone built this world with love.
Joyful Contrast — Mooseville uses bold, saturated color contrast not for shock but for joy. Deep emerald forest with warm gold accents, rich bark browns with cream highlights, sky blue with soft lavender — these combinations must feel delightful, balanced, and intentional.

2. Style Position & References

Primary Reference: Barbie Horse Adventures series — painterly shadow bands, soft warm rim lighting on characters, vibrant natural greens, warm ambient fill, and storybook environment composition.

Style target: ~70% Stylized / ~60% Volumetric. Strong cel influence, non-photorealistic palette, readable silhouettes, with 3D form reading clearly via shadows and rims. Pure cel-shaded games sit at ~100% Stylized / 10–20% Volumetric; AAA photorealistic games at ~10% Stylized / 90% Volumetric.

4. Color Rules

Emerald Depth Rule: All green colors must carry warm undertones — hue must stay between 95° and 130° (yellow-leaning green range) in HSL. Greens drifting above 130° toward cyan are prohibited in all surface albedo, lighting, and UI contexts. When in doubt, add +5° to +10° hue shift toward yellow and increase saturation slightly to restore the signature Mooseville emerald quality.

Gold Accent Discipline: Gold (#FFD700 family) is never used as a base fill for large surfaces. It is exclusively reserved for: (1) specular highlights and rim light tints on characters and props, (2) interactable object outlines and UI accents, (3) hero prop surface inlays and decorative embossing, (4) magic VFX endpoint color, (5) text headers in formal documentation. Any surface larger than ~0.25m² that appears predominantly gold must be revised to a warm cream or bark-brown base with gold highlights overlaid.

Golden Hour Default: Scene ambient always warm (minimum 3200K equivalent). Never neutral grey or cold blue in ambient. Night scenes use deep blue-green ambient but characters retain their warm fill light rig.

10. Outline System

Method: Post-process edge detection — depth buffer + normal buffer differentials. Depth threshold: 0.015 (NDC); Normal threshold: 0.25 (dot product difference); Luminance threshold: 0.18. Outline color: #1A0F00 warm near-black is the darkest allowed — pure #000000 is prohibited in all contexts. TAA smoothing: 4-frame accumulation at 0.25 blend weight per frame (exponential moving average) to eliminate animation flicker.

Skin & Fur Shader Params

Hero characters use a shadow-tint SSS approximation — no separate SSS pass. Shadow band tinted warm amber #6B3A1F gives the appearance of light glowing through ears and muzzle. Fur normal maps follow hair-flow direction; normal strength 0.35–0.50 only (above 0.50 reads as bumpy, not storybook-soft).

NPC characters: no SSS, shadow tint neutral warm #5A4030, standard 2-band.

Eye Shader Rules — MV_Eye
  • Scale: Eyes are 15–20% larger than anatomical reference — deliberate design; must be preserved at all LOD levels including LOD1.
  • Catchlight: Static warm-white (#FFFDE7) texture element baked into emissive at the 10 o'clock position (upper-left iris quadrant). Never derived from real-time lighting; never size-reduced at LOD1.
  • Iris: 3-band cel — dark ring / mid iris / bright center. Smoothstep width 0.01.
  • Pupil: Soft-edged ellipse, 5% warm dark border #1A0F00. No hard edge.
  • Wet rim: Lower eyelid Blinn-Phong, specular power 128, intensity 0.3.

VFX Scale Rules & Prohibited Practices
  • Magic sparkle size: 0.02m–0.15m; always camera-facing billboard.
  • Non-magic VFX color saturation: 0.05–0.25 maximum — warm hue, desaturated.

18. UI Rendering

MV_UI_Flat Shader Rules
  • No depth test — UI always renders on top of scene geometry, after all post-processing.
  • No scene lighting — UI is unlit and self-contained; no bloom, tone mapping, color grading, or vignette applied to UI layer.
  • Bordered panels must use 9-slice scaling — non-9-sliced borders on panels are prohibited.
  • UI composited last in post-processing stack (step 8).

20. Prohibited Shader Practices

PROHIBITED SHADER PRACTICES — Zero Tolerance

All nine items below are hard violations. Any occurrence must be fixed before the asset can be integrated or shipped. Deviations require written approval from the Rendering Director.

  1. No screen-space reflections (SSR) on foliage — SSR on alpha-clipped geometry produces severe artifact bands. Foliage has zero reflections.
  2. No subsurface scattering (SSS) on non-organic surfaces — SSS reserved for primary character skin approximation only. Never on wood, stone, water, or fabric.
  3. No physically-based sky (IBL from raw HDRI) as sole ambient source — IBL must always be color-graded to the golden-hour default. Raw HDRI capture from reference photos is never used directly.
  4. No runtime shader compilation — All variants must be pre-compiled and stripped at build time. No dynamic shader keyword toggling at runtime in any shipped build.
  5. No normal maps on VFX particles — VFX particles use flat color + emissive only. Normal maps on particles waste memory and create visual noise.
  6. No anisotropic specular on character skin — Skin uses single-dot Blinn-Phong only. Anisotropic specular is reserved for hair/mane (MV_Hair_Anisotropic shader exclusively).
  7. No unbounded emissive on environment architecture — Lanterns, glowing fungi, and architectural emissives must use a masked emissive with HDR max 2.0. Unbounded emission causes bloom overload.
  8. No shadow casting or receiving from particle VFX — All VFX particles are excluded from shadow casting and receiving in every rendering context on every platform.
  9. No pure black (#000000) or pure white (#FFFFFF) in any shader output — Albedo floors at 15/255; ceilings at 240/255. Outline darkest value is #1A0F00. All shadow tints must be warm-colored, never neutral black.
