Art Direction

Visual Style Guide

Master color palette, shader settings, lighting profiles, material guidelines, and the visual principles that unify the Mooseville look — a Barbie Horse Trails pastel aesthetic meets warm, nature-grounded Moosian charm.

🎯

Visual Philosophy

Core Principles

  • Soft, not sharp: no harsh edges, no gritty detail
  • Pastel, not neon: colors are bright but slightly milky
  • Simple, not noisy: one idea per asset; avoid micro-detail
  • Readable silhouettes: shapes clear at a glance
  • Consistent outlines: same thickness rules everywhere

Do

  • Use large, clean color blocks
  • Exaggerate curves and round corners
  • Keep texture resolution modest but crisp (512–1024 for most props)
  • Use color/value contrast more than texture detail

Visual References

Barbie Horse Trails aesthetic: soft GI, pastel lighting, smooth surfaces, gentle bloom, minimal roughness variation, friendly cartoon shapes. Combined with Mooseville's identity: emerald green, gold, thick outlines, warm browns.

Don't

  • Use photo textures or scanned materials
  • Use high-frequency noise in normals or albedo
  • Push specular/metallic into realistic territory
  • Let shadows go near-black

Outline Shader (Critical for Mooseville Identity)

Thickness by Asset Type

AssetThickness
Characters2–3 px
Props1.5–2 px
Buildings1–1.5 px
Terrain0–1 px (optional)

Style Rules

  • Color: always #1A1A1A — never pure black
  • Consistent thickness — no jitter
  • No sketchy or hand-drawn lines
  • Same rules apply regardless of distance (scale with LOD)
The consistent outline system is what makes Mooseville feel cohesive and readable — it must be uniform across all asset types.
🌤️

Lighting Profile

☀️

Light Sources

Key Light (Sun)
  • Color: warm — #FFE8C9
  • Intensity: medium-high
  • Shadows: soft, slightly blurred
Fill Light (Sky)
  • Color: cool pastel blue — #CFEAFF
  • Intensity: low
  • No shadows
Rim Light (Optional)
  • Color: slightly warm or neutral
  • Intensity: low — only to help silhouettes pop

Environment Shader Settings

🌲

Trees

  • Leaf clusters: flat color + soft gradient
  • No leaf textures
  • Trunks: smooth, rounded, minimal detail
🪨

Rocks

  • Rounded shapes
  • Very soft normals
  • Roughness: 0.75–0.9
  • Pastel gray-brown palette
🌿

Grass & Ground

  • Flat color cards, slight color variation (±5–8%)
  • No wind noise textures — simple sine-based sway
  • Ground: painterly, soft, low-contrast
  • Use color zones instead of texture detail
🏠

Buildings — Wood

  • Smooth, stylized — very faint plank lines
  • Roughness: 0.65–0.8
  • Roofs: flat color with subtle shading
💧

Water

  • Soft Fresnel effect
  • Roughness: 0.05–0.15
  • Gentle distortion, no harsh refraction
  • Subtle color-coded for meteor zones (green tint)
✨

VFX

  • Sparkles: additive blending, soft pastel colors, bloom-friendly
  • Dust trails: alpha-blended, soft falloff, pastel browns
  • Magic glow: additive + bloom, pastel gold/pink/blue

Color Philosophy

To keep Mooseville consistent:
  • 🟢 Use emerald + gold as your signature brand colors
  • 🌿 Use pastel greens + browns for terrain and props
  • 🔵 Use soft blues for water and sky
  • 🌸 Use pinks + yellows for accents and collectibles
  • ⬛ Use thick outlines (#1A1A1A) for UI and characters
This gives you a world that feels: warm · friendly · magical · consistent · and visually compatible with Barbie Horse Trails.
