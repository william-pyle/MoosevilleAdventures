1. Game Overview & Vision

Elevator Pitch: Mooseville Adventures is a hybrid cozy-adventure game set 22 years after a magical meteor strike awakened a valley of moose into a fully realized civilization. Playing as a grown child of the town's legendary founders — Melvin and Martha — you tend your home, forge bonds with your community, and venture into an ancient and changing wilderness to unravel the mysteries the Starfall left behind. A game about belonging, discovery, and carrying your family's legacy forward.

2. Five Design Pillars

The Mooseville Promise: Every hour should feel like a warm autumn afternoon — productive, peaceful, and full of small moments worth remembering. Challenge comes from curiosity, not combat. Progress comes from relationships, not grinding.

Pillar 1 — Roots and Routes

The player has deep roots in Mooseville (family legacy, known neighbors, inherited home) AND open routes into a wilderness that has never been fully mapped. Both matter equally. Every play session must offer both a grounding village activity and an invitation to explore.

Pillar 2 — Every Moosian Has a Story

NPCs are not quest dispensers. Every named Moosian has a life that continues whether or not the player interacts with them. Relationships deepen through repeated presence, small gifts, shared meals, and remembered conversations — not dialogue trees.

Pillar 3 — The Valley Is Still Changing

The Starfall happened 22 years ago, but the valley is not finished. New awakened moose still arrive. The sealed eastern pass still groans. The Green Spring still glows in ways Mooseville science cannot explain. The player lives on a frontier of understanding.

Pillar 4 — Cozy Has Consequence

Village activities — farming, crafting, cooking, gifting — have real mechanical weight. What the player grows feeds real NPC needs. What they craft unlocks real expedition capabilities. The cozy loop and the adventure loop feed each other; neither is a side dish.

Pillar 5 — Inherited Wonder

Melvin and Martha built something extraordinary. Playing as their child means inheriting that wonder — but also questioning it and ultimately adding to it. The player's own name goes into Mooseville's history by the end. The ending is not a triumph over a villain but a contribution to a legacy.

3. Three-Act Story Structure

Act 1 — "Learning the Town"
Hours 1–8 approx.

The player re-integrates into Mooseville life. Mystery threads are introduced as curiosities stumbled across naturally, not as missions. Key beats: Restore the homestead; meet the current NPC generation; find the private journal page tucked inside the homestead wall; discover the Enhancement Works are weakening. Climax: A tremor reopens a crack toward the sealed eastern pass. Melvin says nothing — but the player catches him looking northeast for a very long time.

Act 2 — "The Valley Opens"
Hours 8–25 approx.

The widest act. Player systematically explores all wilderness biomes, deepens relationships, and pieces together the three mystery threads. Key beats: Restore the Enhancement Works via a new High Shelf sky-stone deposit; uncover human remnant artifacts suggesting the valley was intentionally surveyed; meet new-arrival moose who report a second sealed pass glowing on the far side. Climax: The sealed eastern pass cracks open enough to enter for the first time — the game's most visually iconic moment.

Act 3 — "What The Star Left"
Hours 25–40+ approx.

The player crosses the eastern pass and confronts the full truth of Mooseville's origin. All three mystery threads resolve. Key beats: Discover the full sky-stone origin network and evidence of intentional (not natural) origin; face the central question — does it matter where the awakening came from, if what grew from it was real? Final Choice (all warm, all valid): Keep the pass open and invite connection; share the Starfall origin with all Moosians; or protect the valley's isolation. Final scene: The player character's name is inscribed in the Archive Hall alongside the First Circle.

4. Player Character

The player character is canonically a child of Melvin and Martha, approximately 22 years old at game start. Returning after a short period in a far-valley settlement, they inherit the original homestead near the Green Spring — a fixer-upper full of memories and meaning.

NameFree text entry — confirmed by Martha saying it aloud in the opening sequence
Fur Color5 options: dark brown, warm auburn, sandy blonde, grey-black, classic brown
Antler Style3 options per gender expression (broad palmated, narrow branched, asymmetric)
Starter Clothing3 options including Melvin's signature overalls style; all replaceable through crafting
Gender ExpressionOpen — no mechanical difference; NPCs use chosen name only, no pronoun selection required

Opening line (fully voiced, spoken once by Melvin): "Everything we built, we built because we didn't look away. Don't look away either."

Weekly Loop (Adventure Spine)

  • 1–2 Community Events (festivals, seasonal harvests, public town votes)
  • 1 Main Quest Beat advancing one or more of the three mystery threads
  • 2–4 NPC Side Quests available and rotating (most patient, some time-sensitive)
  • 1 Wilderness Discovery (new area, surfacing artifact, or new arrival moose encounter)
No Fail State: The player cannot die, go broke (basic food and water always free), or permanently miss content. Quests wait patiently. NPCs forgive absence. Time-limited events offer alternative completion paths.

6. Farming System

Farm plot starts at 8 crop slots; expands to 32 across 4 expansions. Applying Moose Water (1 portion per slot) accelerates growth 20% and produces Enhanced Variants — the most powerful gifting items in the game. Optional weeding (neglected plots -30% yield), fertilizer (+50% yield per cycle).

7. Fishing System

Four distinct zones with unique fish populations and musical ambience. Rhythm-based minigame: timing bar, tap to set the hook, hold within a comfort zone to reel. Rare fish require tighter timing. Assisted mode (wider windows, slower indicator) available in settings at any time.

9. Cooking System

The most socially powerful system in the game. A well-made cooked meal is the strongest possible gift — more effective at deepening NPC Closeness than any purchased item. Cooked meals also provide the best in-game stamina and Knack bonuses.

  • Personal cooking station at the homestead (upgradeable alongside the craft station)
  • Public cooking at Marjorie Bell lineage community events (mini-challenges)
  • 40+ recipes unlockable through exploration, NPC gifts, Sacred Document translations, seasonal discovery
  • Seasonal recipes unlock festival participation — some festivals require a specific dish to participate fully
  • Meal benefits: best stamina restoration in game; temporary Knack bonuses (1 in-game day duration); Closeness boost when gifted to NPCs

10. Homestead System

The inherited homestead begins in warm disrepair and transforms into the player's own space over the course of the game. NPCs visit and react to decor — each named NPC has 3–5 unique decor reactions relevant to their character. Place and rotate furniture on a grid; outdoor space includes the personal garden, a small patio, and access to the Green Spring path.

11. Social & Relationship System

Relationships develop through accumulated presence and remembered interactions — never through a dialogue menu or gift-spam loop. Closeness Level is never displayed numerically; it manifests in how NPCs speak to the player, what they share, and where they are willing to go.

LevelPlayer ExperienceGameplay Unlock
1 — FamiliarNPC knows player's name and parentage; warm but general dialogueBasic quests available; NPC location NOT shown on map
2 — AcquaintanceNPC remembers one past interaction; greets with specificityNPC location shown on map; first personal detail shared
3 — FriendNPC initiates conversation; mentions player in dialogue with othersDeeper quests; Chester Lang's advanced crafting unlocked
4 — Close FriendNPC visits homestead; shares personal history; more candid in dialogueHome expansion collaborations; NPC shares exclusive lore
5 — TrustedNPC will accompany player on wilderness visits; offers most personal story arcNPC companion during exploration (passive, flavor-only); deepest quests
6 — Legacy BondUnique "Legacy Moment" story beat triggered — one-time, permanent, emotionally significantLegacy Moment recorded in Archive Hall; unique cosmetic or lore unlock

Remembered Conversations: Up to 5 conversation tags logged per NPC. NPCs reference these tags in future dialogue naturally and unpromptedly — a fishing trip, a crop failure, a discovery in the woods. More remembered conversations produce noticeably warmer, more specific NPC speech.

Closeness Growth: Gifting (cooked meals highest value), consistent presence at NPC locations, quest completion, event attendance, engaging with topics the NPC cares about. Missing events is never penalized — NPCs mention absence warmly with no Closeness reduction.

12. Knack System

Five Knack Trees, each tied to a design pillar. Progression is entirely emergent — driven by what the player actually does, not by spending XP. Each tree has 5 tiers with 3 abilities each (75 total across all trees). Players are not expected to fill all trees in one playthrough. Post-launch New Game+ allows continued development beyond the main story.
