1. Game Overview & Vision

Elevator Pitch: Mooseville Adventures is a hybrid cozy-adventure game set 22 years after a magical meteor strike awakened a valley of moose into a fully realized civilization. Playing as a grown child of the town's legendary founders — Melvin and Martha — you tend your home, forge bonds with your community, and venture into an ancient and changing wilderness to unravel the mysteries the Starfall left behind. A game about belonging, discovery, and carrying your family's legacy forward.

GenreHybrid Cozy Life Sim + Open-World Adventure
Core Fantasy"I am a founding family's child — I know this town, I love this town, and I'm about to discover what lies beyond it."

2. Five Design Pillars

The Mooseville Promise: Every hour should feel like a warm autumn afternoon — productive, peaceful, and full of small moments worth remembering. Challenge comes from curiosity, not combat. Progress comes from relationships, not grinding.

Pillar 1 — Roots and Routes

The player has deep roots in Mooseville (family legacy, known neighbors, inherited home) AND open routes into a wilderness that has never been fully mapped. Both matter equally. Every play session must offer both a grounding village activity and an invitation to explore.

Pillar 2 — Every Moosian Has a Story

NPCs are not quest dispensers. Every named Moosian has a life that continues whether or not the player interacts with them. Relationships deepen through repeated presence, small gifts, shared meals, and remembered conversations — not dialogue trees.

Pillar 3 — The Valley Is Still Changing

The Starfall happened 22 years ago, but the valley is not finished. New awakened moose still arrive. The sealed eastern pass still groans. The Green Spring still glows in ways Mooseville science cannot explain. The player lives on a frontier of understanding.

Pillar 4 — Cozy Has Consequence

Village activities — farming, crafting, cooking, gifting — have real mechanical weight. What the player grows feeds real NPC needs. What they craft unlocks real expedition capabilities. The cozy loop and the adventure loop feed each other; neither is a side dish.

Pillar 5 — Inherited Wonder

Melvin and Martha built something extraordinary. Playing as their child means inheriting that wonder — but also questioning it and ultimately adding to it. The player's own name goes into Mooseville's history by the end. The ending is not a triumph over a villain but a contribution to a legacy.

3. Three-Act Story Structure

Act 1 — "Learning the Town"
Hours 1–8 approx.

The player re-integrates into Mooseville life. Mystery threads are introduced as curiosities stumbled across naturally, not as missions. Key beats: Restore the homestead; meet the current NPC generation; find the private journal page tucked inside the homestead wall; discover the Enhancement Works are weakening. Climax: A tremor reopens a crack toward the sealed eastern pass. Melvin says nothing — but the player catches him looking northeast for a very long time.

Act 2 — "The Valley Opens"
Hours 8–25 approx.

The widest act. Player systematically explores all wilderness biomes, deepens relationships, and pieces together the three mystery threads. Key beats: Restore the Enhancement Works via a new High Shelf sky-stone deposit; uncover human remnant artifacts suggesting the valley was intentionally surveyed; meet new-arrival moose who report a second sealed pass glowing on the far side. Climax: The sealed eastern pass cracks open enough to enter for the first time — the game's most visually iconic moment.

Act 3 — "What The Star Left"
Hours 25–40+ approx.

The player crosses the eastern pass and confronts the full truth of Mooseville's origin. All three mystery threads resolve. Key beats: Discover the full sky-stone origin network and evidence of intentional (not natural) origin; face the central question — does it matter where the awakening came from, if what grew from it was real? Final Choice (all warm, all valid): Keep the pass open and invite connection; share the Starfall origin with all Moosians; or protect the valley's isolation. Final scene: The player character's name is inscribed in the Archive Hall alongside the First Circle.

4. Player Character

The player character is canonically a child of Melvin and Martha, approximately 22 years old at game start. Returning after a short period in a far-valley settlement, they inherit the original homestead near the Green Spring — a fixer-upper full of memories and meaning.

9. Cooking System

The most socially powerful system in the game. A well-made cooked meal is the strongest possible gift — more effective at deepening NPC Closeness than any purchased item. Cooked meals also provide the best in-game stamina and Knack bonuses.

  • Personal cooking station at the homestead (upgradeable alongside the craft station)
  • Public cooking at Marjorie Bell lineage community events (mini-challenges)
  • 40+ recipes unlockable through exploration, NPC gifts, Sacred Document translations, seasonal discovery
  • Seasonal recipes unlock festival participation — some festivals require a specific dish to participate fully
  • Meal benefits: best stamina restoration in game; temporary Knack bonuses (1 in-game day duration); Closeness boost when gifted to NPCs

10. Homestead System

The inherited homestead begins in warm disrepair and transforms into the player's own space over the course of the game. NPCs visit and react to decor — each named NPC has 3–5 unique decor reactions relevant to their character. 11. Social & Relationship System

Relationships develop through accumulated presence and remembered interactions — never through a dialogue menu or gift-spam loop. Closeness Level is never displayed numerically; it manifests in how NPCs speak to the player, what they share, and where they are willing to go.

LevelPlayer ExperienceGameplay Unlock
1 — FamiliarNPC knows player's name and parentage; warm but general dialogueBasic quests available; NPC location NOT shown on map
2 — AcquaintanceNPC remembers one past interaction; greets with specificityNPC location shown on map; first personal detail shared
3 — FriendNPC initiates conversation; mentions player in dialogue with othersDeeper quests; Chester Lang's advanced crafting unlocked
4 — Close FriendNPC visits homestead; shares personal history; more candid in dialogueHome expansion collaborations; NPC shares exclusive lore
5 — TrustedNPC will accompany player on wilderness visits; offers most personal story arcNPC companion during exploration (passive, flavor-only); deepest quests
6 — Legacy BondUnique "Legacy Moment" story beat triggered — one-time, permanent, emotionally significantLegacy Moment recorded in Archive Hall; unique cosmetic or lore unlock

Remembered Conversations: Up to 5 conversation tags logged per NPC. NPCs reference these tags in future dialogue naturally and unpromptedly — a fishing trip, a crop failure, a discovery in the woods. More remembered conversations produce noticeably warmer, more specific NPC speech.

Closeness Growth: Gifting (cooked meals highest value), consistent presence at NPC locations, quest completion, event attendance, engaging with topics the NPC cares about. Missing events is never penalized — NPCs mention absence warmly with no Closeness reduction.

12. Knack System

Five Knack Trees, each tied to a design pillar. Progression is entirely emergent — driven by what the player actually does, not by spending XP. Each tree has 5 tiers with 3 abilities each (75 total across all trees). Players are not expected to fill all trees in one playthrough. Post-launch New Game+ allows continued development beyond the main story.

13. Exploration & Quest Systems

"Every biome must contain at least one thing the player cannot fully understand the first time they find it. The valley earns its depth by rewarding return visits." — Exploration Promise
Exploration Philosophy

The wilderness is not a danger zone — it is a wonder zone. There are no enemies. Obstacles are environmental: stamina barriers, locked areas requiring tools or relationship keys, and seasonal access gates. The reward for exploration is discovery, lore, materials, and new NPC encounters.

Stamina System
ParameterDesign Spec
Trail Stamina BarVisual bar (bottom-left HUD); wood-framed, amber liquid fill; pulses softly when below 20%
Depletion ActionsRunning, swimming, climbing, prolonged sprinting
RestorationResting on any bench/log (5 in-game minutes), eating cooked food, drinking Moose Water
Empty StaminaPlayer reduced to a slow walk — never fully stopped. The player always moves forward.
Trail Knack EffectTier 1: +20% bar. Tier 3: reduced drain rate. Tier 5: swimming costs no stamina.
Non-Negotiable: Stamina reaching zero is never a fail state. Stamina depletion = slowdown only.

Quest Journal & Wilderness Discovery

The Personal Journal is the player's primary quest interface — a hand-drawn, in-character notebook written in the player character's voice. It reads like a diary with purpose, not a task list. Active quests include the player's emotional reaction, not just the objective. Completed quests are marked with a small hand-sketched illustration.

  • Quest markers are optional — a toggle in settings hides all world-space markers for pure exploration players
  • The world map starts partially blank — filled in through player exploration; each new biome entered auto-generates a journal entry
  • Landmark Naming: When the player discovers an unnamed landmark, they can name it. Named landmarks appear on the town map permanently. NPCs reference the player's chosen name in dialogue thereafter
New Arrival Encounter Arc

At certain wilderness locations, the player encounters a recently awakened moose — partially regressed, confused, and in need of help. These are among the game's most emotionally resonant moments.

  1. Approach carefully — the moose is startled; player must move slowly (proximity patience mechanic)
  2. Communicate gently — 3-option choice-based interaction; tone affects encounter but not outcome
  3. Provide Moose Water — costs 1 portion; restores clarity and allows fuller communication
  4. Offer a choice — join Mooseville, or be assisted back to their home territory. The player respects the choice either way.
Up to 8 new arrivals can join Mooseville over a full playthrough. The New Arrivals District grows visibly as each arrival settles in.

15. Economy & Currency

"The economy's purpose is to give meaningful weight to player choices about how to spend surplus — not to create scarcity or pressure." — Economy Philosophy
Mucks — Carved Wooden Coins

Mucks are hand-carved wooden discs with the Green Spring glyph on one face and the founding year on the other. They are exchanged only for non-essential goods and services. Their existence reflects Mooseville's founding values: you trade for the things you want, never the things you need.

The Two Laws — Mechanical Implementation
Law I — Moose Water is Free
The Enhancement Works dispenses a minimum of 1 free Moose Water portion per day regardless of save state, story progress, or resource level. This floor cannot be set below 1 by any system — including the Act 1 degradation event.
Law II — Basic Food is Free
Marjorie Bell's public meal station always has at least 3 free basic food items available per day. This supply cannot be depleted by the player taking it. It is an inviolable civic guarantee.

Green SpringThe original spring where sky-stone shards settled after the Starfall. Still glows green at night. Located near the player's homestead. The physical source of the awakening.
Moose WaterWater enhanced by sky-stone contact through the Enhancement Works system. A free civic resource that reverses awakening regression. Governed by the First Law.
MucksCarved wooden coins; Mooseville's local currency for non-essential goods. Never required for food or Moose Water.
Sky-StonePhysical fragments of the meteor that fell during the Starfall. The source of the awakening's magical substrate. Found scattered in wilderness deposits throughout the valley.
Sacred DocumentsThe books, magazines, field guides, and printed manuals found in the old human campsite. Mooseville's founding cultural artifacts. Preserved in the Archive Hall.
The First CircleMelvin, Martha, and their eight specialist collaborators who founded and shaped Mooseville. Their descendants are the primary Tier 2 NPCs.
Enhancement WorksThe civic system that channels sky-stone influence into the town's water supply, producing Moose Water for public distribution. Critical infrastructure with gameplay significance in Act 1.
The Fading YearsThe crisis period (Year 4–5 post-Starfall) when the green water stream was diverted, causing regression in some Moosians. Mooseville's defining historical trauma.
KnackThe player's progression system. Five Knack Trees (Harvest, Craft, Heart, Trail, Spring) advance through gameplay action rather than XP spending. Each tree: 5 tiers × 3 abilities = 75 total abilities.
Trail StaminaThe player's exploration energy resource. Depletes during running, swimming, and climbing. Never causes a fail state — depleted Stamina reduces the player to a walk, never stops them.
Closeness LevelThe 1–6 relationship depth scale with each named NPC. Never displayed numerically to the player; expressed through NPC dialogue warmth, availability, and quest depth.
Legacy MomentThe special, unique story beat at Closeness Level 6 with a Tier 2 NPC (or at story completion for Tier 1 NPCs). Permanently recorded in the Archive Hall. Cannot be repeated or undone.
Archive HallThe building in the Archive Quarter where Mooseville's history is preserved in writing. The location where the player character's name is formally inscribed at the end of the game.
DailyScheduleComponentThe custom UE5 Actor Component that governs NPC daily routines. Subscribes to the TimeManager's DayPhaseChanged event and moves NPCs to their scheduled locations accordingly.
The Two LawsThe two inviolable civic laws of Mooseville: (1) Moose Water is free, (2) Basic food is free. Mechanically enforced in gameplay — the player cannot be denied either resource.
Appendix B — Design Intention: The Final Word on Mooseville Adventures

Mooseville Adventures is not a game about surviving. It is not a game about conquering. It is not a game about winning.

It is a game about remembering that the best things — the fire that stays lit, the water that stays free, the name that gets carved into stone — are built by people who stayed, who helped, and who looked at the world around them and decided it was worth caring for.

The player carries Melvin and Martha's name. They inherit a town that is already extraordinary. The question the game asks — quietly, warmly, and without judgment — is: what will you add to it?

By the end of the game, they will have an answer. And it will be theirs alone.
