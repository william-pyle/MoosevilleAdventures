Audio Design

Adaptive music system, biome soundscapes, MetaSounds implementation, and the complete sonic identity of Mooseville Adventures.

🎵

Audio Vision & Philosophy

🔊

1.1 — The Mooseville Sound Promise

Every sound in Mooseville Adventures — from the creak of the Gathering Hall door to the full orchestral swell of a Legacy Moment — should feel like it belongs to the same hand-crafted, living world. Audio is not decoration in Mooseville. It is memory. It is the way the player feels the difference between standing in the Emerald Forest at dawn and standing on the High Shelf in a winter wind. Audio is how NPCs feel present even when off-screen. Audio is how the Green Spring communicates that something here is older and stranger than ordinary water.

In Mooseville Adventures, audio is a primary narrative layer — not a secondary feedback system. Every asset that enters this game must earn its place in the world's acoustic identity.
1.2 — Three Audio Pillars
I
"Acoustic First"

Mooseville's world is built by hand with natural materials. Its soundscape must reflect this. The foundation of every biome's ambient layer is acoustic, organic, and real-world recorded. Synthesized elements are layered only where the world requires something beyond nature — the sky-stone, the Green Spring's glow, magical Moose Water interactions.

II
"Music That Breathes"

The musical score of Mooseville is adaptive and layered. It responds to time of day, player activity, emotional narrative weight, season, and biome. Music never starts or stops abruptly — it breathes in and out.

III
"Sound Tells the Story"

Audio cues in Mooseville carry narrative information. The Enhancement Works makes a specific harmonic hum — and when it begins to degrade, that hum subtly changes in a way the attentive player will notice before any quest reveals it. The eastern pass, when the Act 2 crack first opens, makes a sound that does not exist anywhere else in the valley.

Music System

🔀

2.1 — Adaptive Music Architecture

Key design rule: All three layers share the same root key and tempo at all times. Transitions use pre-written stem transitions — not crossfades. No hard cuts. No jarring handoffs.
Layer A
Foundation

Primary harmonic bed; sets tonal center and emotional register.

Control: Biome + Time of Day
Layer B
Activity

Melodic content; responds to what the player is doing.

Control: Player Activity State
Layer C
Moment

Accent layer; swells and flourishes for story beats, discoveries, NPC moments.

Control: Narrative Event Trigger
🎻

2.2 — Instrumentation Palette

Core Instruments — Always Present in Some Form
  • Acoustic guitar (fingerpicked, warm mid-register) — the melodic heartbeat of Mooseville; associated with home, community, and the player character
  • Cello (solo or small section) — emotional depth; associated with legacy, memory, and Melvin/Martha
  • Recorder / wooden flute — lightness, nature, discovery; leads melodic lines in exploration
  • Hand percussion (frame drum, hand pan) — rhythm in active moments; natural and organic
  • Hammered dulcimer — bright, folk-instrument warmth; associated with festival, market, community events
  • String quartet — emotional weight for Legacy Moments and Act climaxes
Textural Elements — Added Contextually
  • Crystal singing bowls — Green Spring presence; sky-stone activation; night ambience
  • Prepared piano (subtle) — human remnant discovery; Ancient Ruins; unsettling-beautiful moments
  • Deep bowed metal (waterphone or similar) — the sealed eastern pass; threshold moments
  • Wordless vocals (single voice, female) — Legacy Moments; Starfall memorial sequences
Instruments Never Used
  • Electric guitar, bass, or synthesizers with obvious digital character
  • Orchestral brass (too martial)
  • Heavy percussion — drums/snare (too aggressive)
  • Pure electronic / EDM textures
Acoustic First: If an instrument is not something that could physically exist in Mooseville's hand-built world, it requires explicit justification to include.
🎵

Biome Ambient Soundscapes

📡

3.1 — Ambient Layer Architecture

Near Layer — 0 to 10m

Footsteps, immediate foliage rustle, water surface, insect detail.

High randomization — varies every 5–30 seconds

Mid Layer — 10 to 50m

Wildlife calls, wind in canopy, flowing water, NPC ambient activity.

Medium randomization — varies every 15–60 seconds

Far Layer — 50m+

Distant wind, mountain echo, thunder, birds in formation.

Low, atmospheric — varies every 60–300 seconds

3.2 — Per-Biome Ambient Specifications
🏘️

Low, harmonic hum (fundamental 80 Hz + 3rd harmonic) audible within 30m of the Shard Station. Pitch descends 2 semitones over 3 in-game days during Act 1 degradation. This is an audio-design narrative signal — the attentive player will hear the problem before any quest flags it.

Character & NPC Audio

👤

4.1 — Player Character Audio

Footsteps

8-material surface system: wood, soil, stone, grass, water, mud, snow, gravel. Minimum 6 variations per material per pace speed. MetaSounds surface query via UE5 physical material tag.

Exertion (Stamina)

Breathing rate increases proportionally with stamina drain; peaks near limit.

Stamina LevelPitch ShiftVolume Shift
50%0.95 rate—
90%1.1 rate—

Eating & Drinking

Distinct sounds per food category (crunchy, soft, liquid); Moose Water carries a distinct reverb shimmer. 4 food categories × 3 variations each.

Crafting

Material-based: wood, metal, fabric, plant. Layered: tool contact + material response.

Swimming

Complete ambient swap; muffled world audio; water movement; breath timing.

DSP: Lowpass filter applied over all world audio at cutoff 800 Hz when the player is submerged. The world sounds muffled, distant, and pressurized.
🗣️

4.2 — NPC Presence Audio

All NPCs produce ambient "presence audio" when within 15m of the player. These sounds make NPCs feel alive even without direct interaction.

Walking (schedule movement) Hoofsteps on appropriate surface Each NPC has a unique gait rhythm
Thinking / idle Soft hum, breath, occasional word fragment Randomized; 1-in-6 chance per 30-second idle period
Eating (meal times) Dish sound; chewing; content exhale Marjorie's station: layered with multiple NPCs simultaneously
Happy (high Closeness, good weather, festival) Brighter vocal ambient; occasional quiet laugh —

Audio Pipeline & Delivery Workflow

📋

8.1 — Audio Production Pipeline

1
Design Brief

Audio Designer writes brief per asset category specifying emotional target, material reference, delivery format, and variation count required.

2
Composition / Recording

Composer or Sound Designer produces source assets in DAW. All deliveries as 48 kHz / 24-bit WAV stems.

3
Review

Audio Lead reviews against the Tone Reference Guide. Any asset conflicting with the acoustic-first philosophy is rejected at this stage.

4
Integration Prep

Assets renamed to naming convention. MetaSound patches built. Parameter hooks confirmed in Blueprint.

5
UE5 Integration

Assets imported into engine. MetaSound Sources configured. Attenuation profiles assigned. Reverb zones verified.

6
In-Engine QA

Full audio QA checklist run in-engine. See checklist below.

7
Approval

Audio Lead sign-off required before content freeze. No exceptions.

✅

8.2 — Audio QA Checklist

  • All files match naming convention
  • No audio clips at 0 dBFS (master peaks at −1 dBFS or lower)
  • No audible loop points on any ambient loop
  • Footsteps trigger correctly on all 8 surface types
  • Music layer transitions occur on Quartz 4-bar boundary
  • Enhancement Works hum pitch shifts correctly with DegradationLevel parameter
  • Sky-stone resonance activates and deactivates cleanly with Spring Knack tier
  • NPC audio signatures identifiable from 10m without visual
  • No audio voice budget exceeded during stress test
  • Reverb zone transitions produce no audio pop or abrupt change
  • Echo Chamber delay parameter verified at 800ms
  • UI sounds pass through without spatialization (confirmed 2D)
  • Seasonal music filter applies and removes without audible artifact
🚫

8.3 — Prohibited Audio Practices

  • No audio asset delivered as MP3 at any stage of production
  • No single-variation footstep systems (minimum 6 per surface)
  • No music that hard-cuts (all transitions must be quantized or faded)
  • No synthetic percussion in any biome ambience
  • No electric or heavily synthesized instrument in the score
  • No UI sound that uses a pure sine wave or harsh digital beep
  • No NPC ambient audio that loops at under 5 seconds
  • No audio volume settings that allow voices to clip without limiting
🔭

Future Audio Considerations

🎙️

9.1 — Voice Acting (Post-Launch)

  • All Moosian voices: warm, natural, slightly unhurried
  • Melvin: low-mid range; deliberate; occasional dry warmth
  • Martha: clear, precise, emotional depth beneath professional tone
  • No cartoony or exaggerated vocal styles
Voice lines will integrate via MS_Voice_NPC. Patch slot is already reserved. All NPC Blueprint components include the voice parameter hook. No architectural rebuild required.
🗺️

9.2 — Dynamic Music Expansion

Future biome additions must extend the EBiomeState enum in BP_MV_MusicManager.

Far Valley music should use the "Lore of the Valley" theme as its foundation, harmonized in F-sharp major — the same harmonic DNA as the Valley theme but a half-step brighter, signaling a world that is related but not identical.

♿

9.3 — Accessibility Audio Pass

  • Visual waveform representation of significant audio cues for deaf/hard-of-hearing players
  • Colorblind-compatible audio cue indicator icons in HUD
  • Full mono compatibility test pass
Design Principle: No narrative-critical information should ever exist exclusively in audio without a visual fallback option.
