Characters & NPCs

The complete NPC Bible — every named Moosian from NPC-001 to NPC-022, wildlife characters, relationship systems, dialogue standards, and the Mooseville idiom reference.

The Closeness Level System

"NPCs are not quest dispensers. Every named Moosian in town has a life that continues whether or not the player interacts with them." — Design Pillar 2
Relationship Mechanics — 6-Tier Closeness Framework

Closeness Level is never displayed as a number to the player. It manifests as changes in how NPCs speak, what they share, and where they are willing to go.

LevelNamePlayer ExperienceGameplay Unlock
1FamiliarNPC knows the player's name and parentage; warm but general dialogueBasic quests available; NPC location NOT shown on map
2AcquaintanceNPC remembers one past interaction; greets with specificityNPC location shown on map; first personal detail shared
3FriendNPC initiates conversation; mentions player in dialogue with othersDeeper quests available; Chester Lang's advanced crafting unlocked
4Close FriendNPC visits the player's homestead; shares personal history; more candid in dialogueHome expansion collaborations; NPC shares lore player couldn't find alone
5TrustedNPC will accompany player on wilderness visits; offers their most personal story arcNPC companion during exploration (passive, flavor-only); deepest quests
6Legacy BondUnique "Legacy Moment" story beat triggered — one-time, permanent, emotionally significantLegacy Moment recorded in Archive Hall; unique cosmetic or lore unlock
Closeness Growth Actions: Gifting (especially cooked meals — highest value) · Showing up consistently during scheduled hours · Quest completion · Event attendance · Remembered conversations (the game logs up to 5 remembered conversation tags per NPC)
Three Core NPC Design Rules
NPCs Remember
Every NPC tracks up to 5 remembered conversation tags. These are topics the player has discussed — a fishing trip, a crop failure, a discovery in the woods. NPCs reference these tags in future dialogue naturally and unprompted. The callback should feel like a friend remembering something, not a system confirming a variable.
NPCs Have Off Days
Named NPCs have mood states driven by story events, anniversaries, and world conditions. A subdued NPC who is quieter than usual is a narrative signal — attentive players notice before any quest reveals the reason.
NPCs Grow
Each NPC has an arc that unfolds across the game's three acts. Their dialogue, availability, and personal concerns evolve based on both story progression and the player's relationship with them. No NPC is static.

Tier 1 — Founding Generation Elders

NPC-001 through NPC-010 · The First Circle + Co-Founders · Story-critical across all three acts

NPC-001 · Tier 1 Elder
Melvin
Town Co-Founder · New Arrival Guide · Informal Spiritual Leader
AgeEarly-to-mid life (moose aging slower after awakening) VisualLarge dark brown moose, grey at muzzle. Teal overalls, red plaid flannel beneath. Gold wheat stalk emblem on chest pocket. Large warm palmated antlers with gentle curve. Moves with quiet authority — never hurried, never still. PersonalitySteady, warm, economical with words. Rarely leaves Mooseville but walks to the Green Spring each evening without fail. He listens more than he talks — but when he speaks, the room quiets. Daily Schedule Dawn — Spring ritual at Green Spring Morning — Growing Circle Midday — Heartfire District Afternoon — Repair Yard Evening — Home with Martha Arc
Letting go. He has built something extraordinary and is learning to step back. His Legacy Moment triggers at game end — not at Closeness Level 6 — when he gives the player the original private journal from the metal trunk. The one he never shared publicly.
Closeness Arc Beats
L1: Welcomes player home; mentions Melvin's Law ("Don't look away")
L2: Shares where the name "Mooseville" came from — a village image in the Sacred Documents
L3: Asks player to help him map the one trail he never finished before the pass sealed
L4: Takes player to the exact place he stood during the Fading Years crisis — says nothing, just stands with them
L5: Tells the player about the night before the First Gathering — what he was afraid of, and what made him walk to the spring anyway
L6 — Legacy Moment: Gives the player the original private journal. The last entry is about the player — written the morning after they returned.
"Everything we built, we built because we didn't look away. Don't look away either."
Voice DirectionDeep, unhurried, warm. A voice that has said important things before and isn't afraid to say them again slowly.
NPC-002 · Tier 1 Elder
Martha
Town Co-Founder · Archive Quarter Lead · Primary Strategist of the Founding
AgeEarly-to-mid life (moose aging slower after awakening) VisualAuburn fur, triple-fork left antler (unique identifier), earth-tone linen clothing, leather satchel always present. Deep green eyes. Ink stain on right hoof — always present regardless of costume. PersonalityWarm but precise. She sees things before others do and asks questions instead of making statements. Will gently challenge the player if they neglect community duties — not with disappointment, but with curiosity. Daily Schedule Dawn — Writing at the Archive Morning — Archive Quarter Afternoon — Community visits Evening — Gathering Hall · then home Arc
Memory and the future. Her Legacy Moment is formally adding the player character's name to the Archive Hall founding record — the game's emotional climax, delivered quietly and with absolute certainty.
Closeness Arc Beats
L1: Hands player the homestead key; says their name aloud — a warm validation moment
L2: Shares the first page of her founding journal — written the night after the First Gathering
L3: Quest: "The Letter Project" — Help Martha compose a diplomatic letter to a far-valley community
L4: Quest: "The Quiet Ledger" — Investigate the Enhancement Works degradation quietly before she raises it publicly
L5: Shares a grief she's carried: she never found out who the humans at the campsite were, or whether they came back
L6 — Legacy Moment: Presents the player with a hand-bound personal journal she has been writing about them since they returned. The last entry is blank — she hands them the pen.
"This is the first thing I wrote. The night after we all sat by the spring and realized we were — this. I wrote: 'We are frightened and we are together and somehow that is enough.' It still is."
Voice DirectionClear, precise, with warmth underneath the precision. A voice that plans ahead and still finds time to laugh.
NPC-003 · Tier 1 Elder
Doris Vale
First Circle — Agricultural Founder · Elder of the Growing Circle
AgeMid-60s (one of the oldest awakened) VisualCompact, wiry moose with sandy brown fur gone silver-white at the tips. Antlers modest and slightly asymmetric — she never minded. Almost always has soil under her hooves. Practical wrap-around aprons over sturdy linen. Moves with unexpected quickness for her age. PersonalityWarm, direct, and deeply practical. No patience for hand-wringing but enormous patience for plants, young people, and anyone willing to learn. Her humor is deadpan and agricultural — comparisons to soil conditions, growth cycles, and harvest timing appear in everything she says about life. Daily Schedule Dawn to midday — Growing Circle (every single day; 22 years without a day off) Afternoon — Nap at home Evening — Heartfire District Arc
Succession. She is ready to pass primary Growing Circle responsibility to her apprentice but has not yet articulated why. The player's involvement helps her name what she is handing over and what it means to let it go.
Key Quest"What Doris Knows" — She takes the player through the entire crop cycle in one session. At the end, she gives them the Moose Water irrigation technique and asks them to promise to teach it to one more person.
"Soil doesn't care about your intentions. It cares about what you put in and what you take out. Same as most things."
NPC-004 · Tier 1 Elder
Raymond Harlow
First Circle — Builder Founder · Semi-Retired Master of Construction
AgeEarly 60s VisualThe largest moose in Mooseville. Enormous frame, dark charcoal fur, wide flat antlers (Grand Palmated). Always wears a thick work belt with hand-carved wooden tools hanging from it. Missing the tip of one ear from a building accident in Year 3 — he tells the story to everyone who asks. PersonalityLoud, generous, and enormously proud of every building in Mooseville. He refers to buildings as "my children" and can recite the construction history of every structure in town. He is sentimental about craft — the difference between a well-jointed corner and a sloppy one matters to him morally. Daily Schedule Morning — Build Yard Midday — Walking tours (inspecting structures) Afternoon — Repair Yard with Chester's lineage Evening — Heartfire Arc
Legacy vs. innovation. His grandchild Pell wants to build something using a new technique from a recovered Sacred Document. Raymond is resistant. The player mediates the generational tension.
Key Quest"New Joints" — Help Raymond and Pell design a hybrid structure combining old Harlow methods with the new technique. The completed building becomes a permanent landmark in town.
NPC-005 · Tier 1 Elder
Gloria Finch
First Circle — Records Founder · Archivist · Keeper of the Wall of Names
AgeLate 50s VisualSlim moose with warm honey-brown fur and reading-glasses perched permanently on her nose (crafted from human remnant lens material and carved wood). Her antlers are neat and relatively small (Tall Crown). Wears layered garments with many pockets — each pocket contains something she needed to remember. PersonalityMeticulous, curious, quietly witty, and absolutely certain that memory is the most important thing a civilization can have. She writes everything down. She is not cold — she is precise. Her affection shows in what she records: she keeps a private journal of kind things she has observed. Daily Schedule Morning — Archive Quarter Midday — Community Board updates Afternoon — Archive Evening — Heartfire (often reads aloud) Arc
The Secure Document Room. She has something locked in there — a translated fragment of human writing that changes the implications of the Starfall. The player earns the right to see it at Level 4. It is the key narrative beat connecting the human remnants to the sky-stone's origin.
Key Quest"The Translated Page" (Level 4) — Gloria has been working for years on one particularly difficult document fragment. The player's help is needed to find a matching fragment in the Ruins. The content is Act 2's central revelation.
NPC-006 · Tier 1 Elder
Harold Winslow
First Circle — Water & Weather Founder · Chief Hydrologist and Meteorologist
AgeLate 50s VisualMedium-build moose with blue-grey fur (unusual coloring; he attributes it to heavy early exposure to Moose Water). Always carries a carved wooden instrument case. His antlers have small carved notches — one for each major weather event he has documented (22 notches). PersonalityMethodical, observant, and quietly passionate. To Harold, water is not just water — it is information. He can read a stream's behavior the way others read a face. He has kept a continuous weather journal for 22 years, never missing a day. Daily Schedule Dawn — Water Station (daily instrument reading) Midmorning — Crystal Lakeshore Afternoon — Rotating field observations Evening — Heartfire Arc
He noticed the Enhancement Works degradation first — three years before anyone else — and has been quietly monitoring, waiting for the right person to tell. The player is that person.
Key Quest"The Long Count" — Harold shows the player his 22-year weather journal and points to an anomaly in sky-stone mineral trace readings that precedes each degradation event. Finding the source requires a High Shelf visit.
NPC-007 · Tier 1 Elder
Marjorie Bell
First Circle — Community Nourishment Founder · Keeper of Shared Meals
AgeMid-50s VisualRound, soft-looking moose (deliberately — the most physically approachable character in the game) with warm cinnamon-brown fur and kind dark eyes. Wears an apron stained by every meal she has ever cooked — she considers the stains a record. Her antlers are wrapped in dried herbs and small flowers. PersonalityThe emotional center of Mooseville's daily life. Marjorie feeds people and in doing so, takes care of them. She remembers what everyone likes to eat. She is the most socially intuitive NPC in the game — the first to notice if the player seems sad or distracted. Daily Schedule Dawn to midday — Public meal station Afternoon — Muckmarket Row Tasting Corner Evening — Heartfire (shares prepared food) Arc
Personal and intimate. She has been cooking for Mooseville for 22 years and recently realized she has never cooked a meal just for herself. The player helps her do that for the first time.
Key Quest"Just For One" (Level 4) — Marjorie asks the player to help gather ingredients for a meal she has always wanted to make but always made for others first. Cooking it together is the quest. The meal is named after her and added to the public recipe board.
NPC-008 · Tier 1 Elder
Chester Lang
First Circle — Repair/Craft Founder · Master of Tools and Salvage
AgeEarly 60s VisualCompact and muscular moose with dark fur worn to near-black from decades of workshop smoke and grease. His antlers have tool loops and small hooks attached — working antlers. Missing the tips of two hooves (industrial incident) with carved wooden prosthetics that click softly when he walks. His eyes are sharp and evaluating. PersonalityEfficient with words, generous with time. Chester believes that a broken thing deserves to be fixed and that fixing things is one of the most profound acts of respect. He is mentoring-oriented — every conversation with him is an indirect lesson. His humor is tool-based: "That argument has a stripped thread." Daily Schedule Morning — Workshop Midday — Salvage Pile review Afternoon — Repair Yard Evening — Heartfire (whittles while listening) Arc
The tool from the original human campsite — the one object he has never been able to figure out the function of. The player discovers what it is (a theodolite — a surveying instrument) and teaches Chester its purpose, unlocking an entire building precision system for the town.
Key Quest"The Unknown Tool" — Identify the theodolite from human remnant fragments and Sacred Document images; return to Chester; unlock precision building options for town projects.
"If it's worth building, it's worth building right. And if it breaks, it was worth building anyway."
NPC-009 · Tier 1 Elder
Evelyn Price
First Circle — Mediator Founder · Retired Conflict Resolution Elder
AgeLate 60s (oldest of the First Circle) VisualThe most elegant moose in Mooseville. Tall, slim, silver-white fur, high narrow antlers (Tall Crown). Moves with deliberate grace. Always dressed in deep forest green with subtle gold-thread embroidery — the only Moosian who consistently wears gold embellishments. Her voice is the calmest sound in any room. PersonalityEvelyn has seen every conflict Mooseville has ever had. She is not passive — she is strategically patient. She knows that most arguments resolve themselves if given time and that most hurt feelings need witnessing before they need solving. She is the character NPCs go to when they are upset. Daily Schedule Morning — Walks perimeter of Mooseville (meditation) Midmorning — Archive Quarter Afternoon — Gathering Hall Evening — Heartfire (most reliably present) Arc
A conflict she failed to resolve in Year 8 — a disagreement about Enhancement Works resource allocation that split one family. She has carried the weight of it. The player helps her make peace with it, not by fixing the old conflict but by documenting it honestly in the Archive.
Key Quest"The Eighth Year" — Help Evelyn compose an honest account of the Year 8 conflict for the Archive; find the two surviving family members and give them the opportunity to add their perspective.
NPC-010 · Tier 1 Elder
Vincent Reed
First Circle — Cartographer Founder · Original Mapper of the Valley
AgeMid-50s VisualWiry, travel-worn moose with rust-brown fur faded at the flanks from years of outdoor exposure. Antlers moderate with a distinctive carved groove running down the center tine — his personal marking. Carries a rolled map case at all times. Boots are always muddy. PersonalityRestless. Vincent is the only member of the First Circle who has never fully settled. He has mapped every part of the valley accessible without crossing the pass — twice. He was the one who argued most strongly, in Year 1, for exploring the eastern pass before the landslide sealed it. He didn't make it in time. This is his great regret. Daily Schedule Variable — rotates field days with archive days Field days — somewhere in the wilderness Archive days — Map Room Arc
Directly tied to the main story. He has spent 22 years perfecting the map of everything that can be reached, waiting for the day something new becomes reachable. When the Act 2 crack opens the eastern pass, Vincent begs to go — but his age and health won't allow it. He gives the player everything: maps, notes, compass, and instructions. His Legacy Moment is learning what the player found on the other side.
Key Quest"The Incomplete Map" — Vincent tasks the player with filling in the only unmapped section of the valley: the High Shelf. Upon returning, the player and Vincent together complete the 22-year map. It is framed and hung in the Gathering Hall.

Tier 2 — Second Generation Heirs

NPC-011 through NPC-018 · Children & grandchildren of the First Circle · The player character's peer group

These characters are younger (20s–40s), fully formed in the town's values, and facing their own questions about what comes next. Their arcs are generally more forward-looking than their elders' — less about preservation and more about expansion, innovation, and identity.

Wildlife Characters

Non-Moosian characters — animals who have not undergone awakening but who have observable personalities and respond to player interaction over time. They operate on a hidden familiarity system independent of the Closeness Level framework. Wildlife interactions are entirely optional and never signposted.

The Bear Family — Thorn, Ember, and Cubs
Location: Emerald Forest northwest

A brown bear pair (Thorn — male, large; Ember — female, slightly smaller) with one or two cubs (season-dependent; visible spring through autumn). Non-threatening. Thorn is territorial about specific foraging areas but not aggressive if approached calmly and slowly.

Familiarity System
Level 1–2: Baseline tolerance — neither approach nor flee
Level 3: Thorn and Ember allow the player to rest near them without moving away
Level 5: Cubs will approach the player directly — one of the most emotionally rewarding optional moments in the game
Lore Connection: Thorn and Ember were present at the First Gathering. They sat at the edge of the group and watched. They were there when Melvin first spoke. Melvin mentions this at Closeness Level 4.
The Eagle Pair (Named by Player at Discovery)
Location: Ancient Ruins / High Shelf cliff faces

Two bald eagles who nest on the ruins plateau and hunt along the High Shelf. At high familiarity, one eagle will periodically circle above the player in wilderness areas and call attention to hidden items or new arrival locations. This mechanic is never explained in the UI — players who invest in the wildlife relationship are rewarded organically.

Player-assigned names appear in the in-game journal and on the natural history illustration in the Archive Quarter, once Gloria's lineage records the pair.

The Beaver Colony
Location: Marsh Shallows western edge

A beaver colony maintaining two active dams. Their dam maintenance sustains the marsh water level — an ecology fact Harold Winslow discusses. If the player accidentally damages a dam, Harold's lineage NPC notices and a small community repair quest activates automatically.

Dialogue Writing Guidelines

Voice and Register
  • All Moosian dialogue is warm, slightly formal in grammar (they learned language from reading; their speech patterns reflect written-to-spoken translation), and rich in nature-and-community metaphors.
  • No Moosian uses idioms borrowed directly from human culture. All idioms are Mooseville-invented.
  • Moosian speech never uses the word "human" — they refer to campsite occupants as "the old visitors" or "the ones from before."
  • Vocabulary draws from seasonal observation, water behavior, soil conditions, sky events, and tool metaphors.
  • Even the most casual NPC speaks with quiet care. The town's speech has a settled, gentle quality — as though everyone is slightly aware that language is a gift they are still learning to use well.

Remembered Conversation Tags — Technical Spec

Each NPC tracks up to 5 remembered conversation tags set by player dialogue choices. Tags are referenced in future dialogue using the syntax below and resolve to contextual callback phrases provided at implementation time.

Tag Syntax: [REMEMBER: tag_name] → inserts contextual callback phrase

Example tags: PLAYER_FISHES_MORNING · PLAYER_MISSES_HOME · PLAYER_CURIOUS_ABOUT_PASS · PLAYER_TALKED_ABOUT_MELVIN
Tags are set silently at dialogue branch selection and persist across sessions. If all 5 tag slots are filled, the oldest tag is replaced. Writers should treat the tag as an opportunity for the NPC to demonstrate genuine attentiveness — not as a data readout. The callback should feel like a friend remembering something, not a system confirming a variable.
